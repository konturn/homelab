A small collection of poems written over the years.

Ground Zero

If this shade could be weighed even Stars would be ashamed for nothing is so heavy as Nothing, as itself, as Thousand suns flash earthbound throwing Ashen shadows etched in Splintering panes bend back Black rains Falling out cloud nine, Fungal phallic leviathan, Literally Deconstruction, Loveless marriage of vision and myopia. Zero use torqueing a lonesome gear Rolling boulders or grasping apples when nothing is so heavy as Nothing, this self.

It's Always Breakfast Somewhere

As the ethereal orb in the placid sky Leaves from American lands, It casts its own benevolent head Upon the Australian sands. One world to sleep, another awakes, And here is our great lesson: Whilst some of us sleep, others eat, For their breakfast is in session.

Untitled

Truth is, I call headlights moonbeams And spin threads out of thin air until Deceptions fade into vague feelings of guile, Whose ineffability slates their perception to defeat. I see rainbows in a penny and my love is in bits: Where one plus one is void, It was a negative sum game after all I will not stop at anything until I bask in that moment black fades to orange and sunlights walk on ice Three birds sing an asbestos melody, Three frames twine hope to myopia. First alien erudition, and lovebound premonitions. dilly-dally stare, limpid indifference, the upright walk and those tumbling words. Until my heart skirts the curve of your Cheshire smile, And I am all yours, and only for a moment Dancing in this blip, I cried bloody buckets This malevolent memory Washed away to a blurred pastel...
